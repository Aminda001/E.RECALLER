Present Tenses

Simple Present Tense

When to Use

Habits, general truths, and permanent situations

Structure

  • Singular: V1 + s/es (He works)
  • Plural: V1 (They work)

Examples

She teaches English daily.

Water boils at 100°C.

Present Continuous Tense

When to Use

Actions happening now or temporary situations

Structure

  • am/is/are + V-ing

Examples

I am studying right now.

They are playing football.

Present Perfect Tense

When to Use

Completed actions with present relevance

Structure

  • have/has + V3

Examples

She has finished her work.

We have seen that movie.

Present Perfect Continuous Tense

When to Use

Ongoing actions that started in the past

Structure

  • have/has been + V-ing

Examples

I have been waiting for hours.

It has been raining all day.

Past Tenses

Simple Past Tense

When to Use

Completed actions in the past

Structure

  • V2 (past form)

Examples

She went to school yesterday.

They played football last week.

Past Continuous Tense

When to Use

Actions in progress at specific past time

Structure

  • was/were + V-ing

Examples

I was sleeping at 10pm.

They were studying when I called.

Past Perfect Tense

When to Use

Actions completed before another past action

Structure

  • had + V3

Examples

She had left before I arrived.

They had finished dinner by 8pm.

Past Perfect Continuous Tense

When to Use

Duration before something in the past

Structure

  • had been + V-ing

Examples

I had been working for 3 hours.

They had been waiting since morning.

Future Tenses

Simple Future Tense

When to Use

Actions that will happen in the future

Structure

  • will + V1

Examples

I will call you tomorrow.

They will arrive at 8pm.

Future Continuous Tense

When to Use

Actions in progress at future time

Structure

  • will be + V-ing

Examples

I will be flying to London this time tomorrow.

They will be having dinner at 8pm.

Future Perfect Tense

When to Use

Actions completed before future time

Structure

  • will have + V3

Examples

By 2025, I will have graduated.

They will have finished by 5pm.

Future Perfect Continuous Tense

When to Use

Duration before future time

Structure

  • will have been + V-ing

Examples

By 2025, I will have been working here for 5 years.

They will have been traveling for 3 months.
